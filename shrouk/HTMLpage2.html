Spongypeon

Igredientes:
2 cups wet rice powder per batch (about 16 songpyeon)
4 to 5 ounces pine needles rinsed 2 – 3 time
1 teaspoon sesame oil
2 cups wet rice powder thawed to room temperature if frozen
4 tablespoons boiling hot water more as needed, in 1 teaspoon increments
1.5 to 2 tablespoons mugwort
⅓ cup roasted sesame seeds
pinch salt

preparing steps:

Step1

For each type of dough, sift the rice powder into a bowl.
Prepare the flavoring and liquid ingredients (water/juice/mugwort powder/kabocha) for each selected color of dough.
Add the specified amount of the solid and/or liquid (making sure it’s<
boiling hot) to the rice powder

Step2

Knead, pressing and stretching with the heel of the hand.
Fold and rotate the dough. Repeat the kneading process for 4 to 5 minutes.
(If the dough sticks to your hands or the bowl after some kneading, it’s too wet.
Add more rice powder in 1 tablespoon increments. If the dough breaks
easily or is too stiff, it’s too dry.

Step3

Roughly grind the sesame seeds in a grinder or blender. Add the
sugar, honey and pinch of salt. Mix everything well together until the
sugar and honey are evenly distributed.

Step4

To assemble, tear off a small piece of the dough enough to make roughly
1-inch ball. Roll tightly between your palms to shape a ball.

Banana flavored milk

Igredientes:
1 ripe banana, sliced or broken into pieces
1 ripe banana, sliced or broken into pieces
1/2 cup water
1 Tbsp maple syrup
A small drop of pure vanilla extract

preparing steps:

Step1

Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth and has milk like consistency.

Step2

If you decide to omit the water and add more regular milk, you will end up with a banana smoothie. (A lot thicker texture.)

Step3

You can use honey instead of maple syrup, but I preferred the outcome with maple syrup more.

Step4

To go dairy free, replace 1/2 cup milk with water instead. Alternatively, you could use
almond milk, but I personally didn’t think the flavor mingled well together.
